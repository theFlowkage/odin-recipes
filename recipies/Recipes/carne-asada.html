Carne asada tacos are a delicious and popular Mexican dish made with grilled and thinly sliced beef, typically marinated in a flavorful mixture of spices, citrus juices (such as lime or orange), garlic, and herbs. The beef used in carne asada is usually flank steak, skirt steak, or a similar cut that is well-suited for grilling. but today we will not be doing it traditional we will be throwing a southern spin on this mexican calssic

BEST CARNE ASADA TACOS EVER

Ingredients

  • One 16oz ribeye
  • 2 bunches of cilantro
  • 1 tomato
  • 4 jalepenos with the seeds removed
  • 2 red onions
  • 1 lemon
  • 4 limes
  • 3 cups of ranch
  • 1 clove of glarlic
  • 2 sauted green bell peppers
  • 1 bag of shredded pepperjack cheese
  • 1 cup vinager
  • flour tortillas
  • butter
  • taco seasoning of your choice
  • 1 pack of bacon

Making the Sauce

  1. Roughly chop 3 jalapenos and cut 2 limes in half
  2. Pour the three cups of ranch, the jalepenos and one one bunch cilantro into a blender
  3. Add tequila lime seasoning
  4. Squeze lime juice
  5. Pulse blend for 30 - 40 seconds

Prepping the Pico

  1. dice 1 half of the red onion, the last jalapeno, and the tomato
  2. cut 1 lime and 1 lemon in half
  3. throw the diced vegtables in a bowl and squeeze the whole lime and one half of the lemon into the same bowls
  4. mix all ingredients together with a pinch of salt, a table spoon of chili powder, and tequila lime seasoning

    5. Pickled onions

    1. bring equal parts water and vinager to a boil
    2. while waiting for the mixture to boil slice 1 whole red onion
    3. peel half a clove of garlic
    4. after the mixture comes to a boil add garlic, onions and red pepper flakes to the mixture and pour into a mason jar and let sit for an hour

    Cooking the Ribeye

    1. let the ribeye rest for and hour on the counter
    2. bring pan to a medium high heat the add olive oil to the pan
    3. pat the ribeye dry with a paper towel and then cover the steak in your prefered taco seasonings, personally i make my own
    4. sear the ribeye on both side then remove it and let rest for another 5 minutes
    5. throw butter seasonings and the othe half of the garlic clove into a pan and bring to a simmer
    6. place steak back on to the pan and baste untill the steak reaches 130 degrees enternal
    7. take the steak off the pan and let rest for five minutes before slicing into the steak
    8. cut steak into thin strips

    Assembling the Taco

    layer the tortilla in this order

    1. spinach
    2. steak
    3. pickled onions
    4. pico
    5. sauce
    6. cilantro
    7. sauted bell peppers
    8. cheese
    9. bacon
